Lasagna

What you need:

  • 300 gram of minced meat, non of that low fat stuff
  • A jar of pasta sauce, 400 grams should be a good amount
  • 3-5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 Onion, also chopped
  • Vegetables, go nuts. I have not yet met a vegetable that does not go well in a lasagna. I am personally found of peppers, spinach and mushrooms. Just make sure it is chopped up
  • Dried lasagna sheets
  • Italian spices and salt
  • Cheese. Don't limit yourself here, cheese you measure with your heart, not a scale. If you are not cheese person, a medium cheddar is a perfect choice.
  • Big Oven Dish
  • Frying Pan
  • Oven
  • Aluminum Foil

What to do?

  1. The step before all steps, pre-heat your oven to 200 degrees celcius. It can take between 10 and 20 minutes for your oven to heat up.
  2. As the oven is heating up, prepare your ingredients, make sure you have everything measured out, cut up and ready to use.
  3. First we brown the onions in our frying pan. Put it on medium and leave it there. Good things take time.
  4. Once the onions are soft and glassy looking we can add the finely chopped garlic. Stir well, this stuff burns fast.
  5. Push the onions and garlic to the side and put in the minced meat. Push it flat in the pan and leave it for a bit. We want it to turn brown before we move it. The brown is the natural sugar of the meat caramilizing and making it extra tasty.
  6. Once the meat is no longer raw put in your assortment of vegetables.
  7. Keep stirring for about 15 minutes. Throw in the pasta sauce, salt, and spices.
  8. It might look very soupy, that is fine. We the lasagna sheets will absorb the liquid while it cooks. Make sure everything is hot but not cooking.
  9. It is time to start building the layers. A big serving spoon works well. Put a thin layer of sauce and cover it by the pasta sheets. Make sure the sheets do not overlap.
  10. Repeat this process untill you are out of sheets/sauce or space. Make sure to finish with a layer of sauce.
  11. This is the most crucial part. Put the cheese on top of the Lasagna. Cover everything. Everything.
  12. Cover the whole thing with aluminum foild and slide it in the oven.
  13. Cook it for 20 minutes with the aluminum foil on top, remove the foil and cook for 20 more minutes.
  14. Now comes the hard part. The lasagna looks crispy and ready to eat. But the truth it, it needs a little rest. Give it 10-15 minutes to cool and the excess liquid to be soaked up by the pasta.
  15. That's it! Cut, serve and enjoy.

Extra info

Feeling fancy? Make different layers in your lasagna. A bechamel sauce or some spinach will give a really nice contracts in your layers.
Pro tip: Lasagna tastes better the next day. I don't know what kind of magic that is, but it just does.
